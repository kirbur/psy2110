There are 3 main types of knitting needles. Straight needles are the most commonly depicted needles, if you have seen a movie where someone is shown knitting, you probably saw them holding 2 straight needles. The other 2 most commonly used needles are double pointed needles (DPNs) and circular needles (circs). Both of these types of needles are used for knitting “in the round”. Knitting in the round means knitting in spirals to create a tube, it is most commonly used for hats, socks, mittens, and the like. Double pointed needles are used for smaller circumference knitting like socks and mittens. Circular needles can be used for any circumference circular knitting as long as the cord l and needle length is just right. Needles are typically made of 3 different materials: wood, metal, or plastic. The metal needles are typically the slickest and are best for experienced knitters. The plastic are more of a medium slickness. If the wood needles are polished then they are very slick otherwise the are the least slick.

Any of the 3 types of needles can be used to knit flat pieces, many knitters find it convenient to use circular needles because they are just one piece and can be easier to keep track of.

Other helpful tools for knitting:

  • Crochet hooks can be used to fix small errors in knitting, like dropped stitches.
  • Tapestry needles are used to weave loose ends in and to sew pieces of knitted fabric together.
  • Stitch markers are used with more complicated patterns to know where to place design elements.
